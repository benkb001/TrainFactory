**** PLAYTESTING **** 

This is to validate players want to play the game, and that 
systems aren't obsolete. 

* Get friends to play it on stream, record these sessions. Some 
  where they talk while they play, some where they talk after. 
  Ask them to explain their thought process while playing the game, 
  how they felt while playing it, if they enjoyed it. Follow up 
  a week later and ask if they played the game any longer, outside of 
  the initial session. 
* Use Player Engagement Survey or similar measurements 
* Record play data and do data analysis on it
  * Measure how long a play session lasts, the time between 
    sessions, how far into the game the player makes it, 
    how much time they spend playing before fully dropping off. 
    Do players make it past the tutorial? Where is the most common 
    stopping point? 
  * Measure how long an individual 'run' is, both in time 
    and how far they make it
  * Measure which upgrades get picked most often
* Find a way to measure the quality of player decisions over time, 
  we want the player to be learning/improving as they progress 
* Measure which systems they're engaging with- are they actually 
  programming their trains? Are they actually upgrading them or just 
  making new ones? Are they unlocking new cities? Are they even 
  upgrading their machines much? Once embassy and settlement are introduced, 
  are they even engaging with them? 
* Measure how often player is making meaningful resource decisions over 
  time. Do they start coasting after programming is unlocked or not? 
* Measure how much players engage with NPCs, if they watch or skip cutscenes, 
  if they complete quests. 
* Measure resource bottlenecks- are players making smart RM decisions, 
  but still ending up with a certain resource scarcity that leads to 
  a longer period of stagnation?
